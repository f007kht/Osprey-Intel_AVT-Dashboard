Founders & Team

The experienced leadership team driving the development of the Autus Valve.

Dr. Hofferberth

Dr. Hofferberth co-founded Autus Valve after conceiving and designing the company's novel technology as a post-doctoral fellow at Boston Children's Hospital. As CMO, she led all pre-clinical studies, secured FDA approval for the first-in-human implant, and achieved both Breakthrough Device Designation and unconditional FDA approval for the U.S. Pivotal Study. She has taken a leave from her cardiothoracic surgery residency at Harvard Medical School to lead the company.

Dr. del Nido

Dr. del Nido is Chairman of Cardiac Surgery at Boston Children's Hospital and the William E. Lads Professor of Surgery at Harvard Medical School. A renowned surgeon and serial entrepreneur, he is the inventor of del Nido Cardioplegia, a global standard for heart preservation in open-heart surgery. He has co-founded multiple medical device companies and holds 28 patents, underscoring his extensive track record in medical innovation.

Dr. Edelman

Dr. Edelman is a distinguished professor at both MIT and Harvard Medical School. A pioneer in vascular biology, he has developed critical clinical devices, including endovascular implants and cardiovascular support systems. He directs the Harvard-MIT Biomedical Engineering Center, has mentored hundreds of leaders in the field, and is a fellow of multiple National Academies, highlighting his immense influence on translational research and medical innovation.

Christine Heyninck-Jantz, BS, MBA

Sr. Vice President, Product Development

Christine is a veteran in transcatheter heart valve development with over 20 years of experience at Edwards and Medtronic. As VP of R&D at Edwards, she pioneered the development and commercialization of the world's first transcatheter valve and successfully launched the Alterra Adaptive Prestent and Sapien 3 Pulmonic Delivery System. She now leads product development and manufacturing at Autus.

VC Investment Insights

Qualitative due diligence summary assessing the management team, market opportunity, and technology.

Venture capital due diligence focuses on three core pillars: the team, the market, and the product. For a pre-revenue company like Autus Valve, the strength of the management team is often the most critical factor, as they must navigate immense scientific and commercial challenges.

1. Management Team

The team is led by CEO Sophie-Charlotte Hofferberth, MD. Her medical background is a significant asset, as evidenced by her dual role as the Study Director for the company's pivotal clinical trial. This indicates deep technical and clinical expertise at the leadership level, which is crucial for credibility with investors, regulators, and clinical partners in the medtech space.

2. Market Opportunity

Autus Valve is targeting the pediatric congenital heart disease market. The primary challenge for patients is the need for repeated, high-risk open-heart surgeries as they outgrow their initial valve implants. Autus aims to solve this critical unmet need. The viability of this market is validated by significant VC investment in similar companies. For example, Xeltis, which also develops restorative cardiovascular devices, has raised nearly $200 million. Other competitors like Polyvascular and Starlight Cardiovascular are also VC-backed, demonstrating a strong and continued investor appetite for innovative solutions in this field.

3. Product & Technology

The company's core product is the Autus Size-Adjustable Pulmonary Valve, a fully synthetic implant. Its key technological differentiator is its ability to be expanded via transcatheter balloon dilation as the child grows, potentially eliminating the need for subsequent surgeries. The company has progressed beyond the concept phase and is actively recruiting for its "Autus Valve Pivotal Study" as of early 2024, a major de-risking milestone that moves the product closer to regulatory review and commercialization.
